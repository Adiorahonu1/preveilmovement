Her Story

FROM BROOKLYN
TO BUILDING
A MOVEMENT

"I never want my family to be separated because of money again."
01

I was born in Brooklyn, New York, after my parents immigrated from Ecuador, chasing the American dream. They worked super hard, always showing me what it meant to hustle. But when I was in second grade, things got tough. We could not afford to stay in the U.S., so we went back to South America. Even as a kid, I knew that financial freedom was something I needed to figure out.

02

Like many, I followed the classic success formula: go to school, get good grades, land a good job. I went into the medical field. But it did not take long to realize that a degree does not guarantee financial security. Even doctors and nurses did not always understand money. That is when I started asking: is this going to be my future too?

03

Then came the moment that changed everything. Someone asked how many languages I spoke. Proudly, I said two: Spanish and English. They shot back: you are missing the most important one. The language of money. I was taken aback, but they were right. If I wanted to get ahead, I had to learn how money really worked.

Mentored by

PATRICK BET-DAVID

Founder of PHP Agency & Valuetainment

@patrickbetdavid
04

A friend invited me to a meeting. After six months of hesitation, I finally went and that is where I met Patrick Bet-David. He became my mentor and completely shifted my perspective on life and business. I founded The Prevail Movement, and together we have helped over 118,000 families protect their legacies and retirements. I believe everything rises and falls on leadership. It is not about the money. It is about who you become along the way.
